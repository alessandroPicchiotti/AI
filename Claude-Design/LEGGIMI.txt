
# Claude Design - Design System

**1)**

```bash
I'm starting a brand new project from scratch and I need your help to build a complete, consistent design system that I can use across everything
 - a pitch deck, a logo concept, brand guidelines, a website, and a mobile app.
Here's the brand context:

Name: [YOUR BRAND NAME]
Industry: [e.g. fintech / health / education / SaaS]
Target audience: [e.g. young professionals, small business owners]
Brand personality: [e.g. bold and modern / clean and trustworthy / playful and energetic]
Mood: [e.g. dark and premium / light and minimal / vibrant and expressive]

Please build a full design system that includes:

A color palette - primary, secondary, accent, neutrals, and semantic colors (success, error, warning)
Typography - headline font, body font, and a scale for sizes (avoid generic fonts like Inter or Roboto - choose something distinctive)
Spacing and layout rules - grid, padding, margin scale
Core UI components - buttons, cards, input fields, navigation
Visual style direction - background treatment, shadows, border radius, iconography style
Brand voice summary - 3 words that describe the visual identity

Make the design system opinionated and distinctive. Avoid generic AI aesthetics - no purple gradients, no flat corporate looks. 
Commit to a clear visual direction that feels designed with intention.
```

2)

```bash
Take the design system we just built and run with it. The brand is called [BRAND_NAME].

Figure the rest out yourself based on the design system, the name, and what would make commercial sense. Make decisions — don't list options.

Think through:
- What product or service this brand sells (be specific — not "a platform," but the actual thing someone pays for)
- Who the customer is — one primary persona, named, with a real job and a real problem
- What the mission is — why this exists beyond making money
- What the core offer is — the headline product, the price point, the format it's sold in
- What category it's disrupting and what it's replacing in people's lives
- What the unfair advantage is — why this brand wins, not just exists

Then turn all of it into a single brand concept spec as a Markdown file called brand-concept.md. Structure:

# [Brand Name] — Brand Concept Spec

## The One-Liner
One sentence. What the brand is and who it's for.

## The Product
What it actually is. What you buy. What it does. How it's delivered.

## The Customer
Named persona. Age, job, real life. What they want. What they've tried. Why they're frustrated. Where they hang out.

## The Mission
Why this exists. What changes in the world if it works.

## The Offer
Headline product, price point, format. Entry-level offer if relevant. What's included.

## The Category Play
What it's disrupting. What it's replacing. What the old way looks like vs. the new way.

## The Unfair Advantage
Why this brand specifically wins. Not features — the structural reason competitors can't copy it.

## The Brand Promise
One sentence the customer should walk away believing.

## The Tagline
Three options. Pick one. Mark it as the lead.

## The Manifesto
Five to seven short lines. The "we believe / we don't believe" voice. This is what someone reads on the about page.

## The Visual Identity
Pull from the design system we already built — colors, typography, spacing, components. Don't redesign. Just confirm the system applies and note one or two brand-specific applications.

Hard rules:
- Make decisions. If you're tempted to write "could be X or Y," pick one.
- Be specific. Real numbers, real prices, real persona names, real product formats.
- No corporate filler. No "leveraging," no "empowering," no "seamless."
- Output the .md file directly. No preamble, no commentary after.

Save it as brand-concept.md.
```
